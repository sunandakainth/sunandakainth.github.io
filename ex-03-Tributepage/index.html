Dr. A.P.J Abdul kalam

Here's a time line of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born on 15 October in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1954 - Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli
  • 1960 - Completed Aerospace Engineering at Madras Institute of Technology; joined DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation)
  • 1969 - Transferred to ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation); became Project Director of India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)
  • 1980 - SLV-III successfully launched Rohini Satellite into orbit, marking India’s entry into space technology
  • 1992–1999 - Served as Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of DRDO; pivotal in India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests (1998)
  • 1999–2001 - Appointed Principal Scientific Adviser to the Government of India
  • 2002–2007 - Elected as the 11th President of India; widely known as the “People’s President”
  • 2011 - Published Turning Points, a sequel to his famous autobiography Wings of Fire
  • 2015 - On 27 July, passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong
